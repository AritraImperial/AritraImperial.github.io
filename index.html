Problems by me

Below are some problems which got proposed by me and were used in various contest, for now I will write down my some FE problems Find all solutions for $f:\mathbb{Z}\rightarrow\mathbb{Z}$ satisfying $$f(y+f(x))+f(x+f(y))-2=f(3-y)+f(3-x)$$ The problem has no solution but that does not makes the problem, let $P(x,y)$ denote the equation. First, $P(x,x)$ implies that $f(x+f(x))=f(3-x)+1$. This implies that for any $a\in R_f$, we must also have $a+1\in R_f$ (here, $R_f$ is the range of $f$). Thus, $R_f$ must either be of the form $[a,\infty)\cap \mathbb{Z}$ for some $a\in\mathbb{Z}$ or $\mathbb{Z}$. We will prove that it can only be $\mathbb{Z}$. For the sake of contradiction, assume that $R_f$ has minimum element $a$. Thus, there exists $k\in\mathbb{Z}$ such that $f(k)=a$. $P(x,y)+P(3-x-f(y),y)$ implies that $f(y+f(x))+f(y+f(3-x-f(y)))-4=2f(3-y)$. Plugging in $y\to 3-k$ gives $f(3-k+f(x))=2a+4-f(3-k+f(3-x-f(3-k)))\le a-4$. Since $3-k+f(x)$ can be any integer not less than $3-k+a$, we get that $a\le f(x)\le a+4$ for all $x\ge 3-k+a$. For any $y\ge 3-k$, we would have $y+f(x)\ge 3-k+a$ and $y+f(3-x-f(y))\ge 3-k+a$. Thus, by the previous equation and bound, we cound get $2a-4\le 2f(3-y)\le 2a+4$ for any $y\ge 3-k$. In particular, we have $f(y)\le a+2$ for any $y\le k$. This means that $f^{-1}([a+5,\infty)\cup\mathbb{Z})$ must be a finite set, at every element in it must be larger than $k$ but smaller than $3-k+a$. However, since $[a+5,\infty)\cup\mathbb{Z}$ is a subset of the range, its pre-image must be infinite, a contradiction. Thus, $f$ is surjective.
